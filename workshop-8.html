Visual Thinking Workshop

Boon Chew

18 de Noviembre

09:00 - 13:00

English

Quiero inscribirme

Every day, we are increasingly exposed to more complexity that test the limits of our abilities and tools. Visual thinking allows us to deal with this in many ways, all with a pen and paper (lots and lots of paper). This sounds all too simple, but if you’ve ever struggled in drawing objects and concepts, illustrating complex diagrams, or sketching a wireframe by hand, then this workshop might be for you.

In this workshop, you’ll learn the basics of how and what to draw, how to apply your skills to complex scenarios, and use your new skills alone and with other people. You’ll also learn how to apply visual thinking towards a the design of omni-channel services.

The session is aimed at designers of all levels and curious individuals wanting to learn new ways to solve problems effectively and collaboratively in an impactful way. No drawing skills necessary!

Key takeaways

  • Drawing techniques for thinking, concepting, modelling and communicating
  • Improving handwriting for clarity and impact
  • Visual thinking for individual work and as a team
  • How to develop your visuals for impact and reach
  • Applying visual thinking at different scales and stages using a service design case study

Workshop Objective

To provide attendees with tangible experiences and approaches of applying visual thinking to solve problems around the design of products and services. Attendees will walk away with an understanding of using visuals to solve problems and communicate solutions. The key outcomes of the workshop are aimed at helping audiences in:

  • Flexibility in thinking
  • Dealing with complexity and ambiguity
  • Developing a visual vocabulary
  • Working visually as a team

What this workshop doesn’t cover

  • Advanced drawing skills
  • Graphic recording / Sketching live on stage
  • Sketchnoting / Sketching live to a presentation or talk

Audience

  • A mix of UX designers, Product managers, Engineering / technical, Startup founders
  • Looking for very practical things - walk out and apply
  • There is an appetite for: strategy, facilitating, design thinking, more detailed skills, service design from different perspectives, running projects and teams, soft skills, business-side of design, working with clients and stakeholders, different angles of design

Requirements

  • No drawing skills or equipment necessary
  • Be curious and keep an open mind
  • Be willing to try new things
  • Be willing to work nicely with others
  • Be willing to share and learn as a group

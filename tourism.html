Currency

The official currency in Argentina is the peso, whose symbol is $ and can be displayed with the ISO 4217 code as ARS. Banknotes in circulation are: $ 2, $ 5, $ 10, $ 20, $ 50 and $ 100. The coins are: 5, 10, 25, 50 cents; $ 1 and $ 2.

You can check the official exchange rate against other foreign currencies on the website of Banco Nación: http://www.bna.com.ar/ or a currency exchange store. Due to limitations on the use of foreign currency in our country, there is a parallel market for foreign exchange, which by the way is illegal. That is why we want to warn you not to transact with people or companies working in this market, even if it may seem like a more attractive offer. Our recommendation is to make exchanges in banks or entities authorized to do it, which can be found around the San Martin Square in the center of the city of Cordoba.

If you need to have pesos upon arrival at the airport, you will find a stand on the same airport. Always check the final price as each exchange company charges a commission.

Power

Power Outlets in Argentina are of type I, although in some older installations you can find some type C, it's also common to find outlets / plugs that are combined C + I.

Before connecting any device to the power line, verify that it supports the voltage. The voltage used in our country is 220 V and 50 Hz frequency, almost all devices currently support it, but it does not hurt to make sure before connecting.

More information about the different outlets and their respective voltages: http://www.viajo.org/mapa-de-tomacorrientes-en-el-mundo

Transport

There are 3 ways of public transport in the city of Cordoba:

Bus: Called locally as "collective", consist of corridors identified by color, each line on a corridor performs a specific route and is identified numerically.
Taxi:Yellow and work the same way as in almost any city in the world, you can stop them on the street.
Remis:It consists of green colored cars and their operation is similar to those of taxis, except that the only way is to get a ride is by a phone call, because they are forbidden to pick up passengers in the street like taxis do.

Day rate

Taxi: $14,70 first 110m, after that $0,73 every 100 meters
Remis: $15,3 first 100m, after that $0,68 every 100 meters

Nightly rate (from 11:00pm to 6:00am

Taxi: $16,15 first 110m, after that $0,80 every 100 meters
Remis: $16,8 first 100m, after that $0,75 every 100 meters

In our country it is not customary to give tip the driver, so you should expect your change. If the driver says not to have change, it's his responsibility to providing it, otherwise they should round the fare for the benefit of the passenger.

To and from the airport

There are two ways to get to and from Ingeniero Ambrosio Taravella International Airport: bus or taxi, the latter being the most recommended and safe. While the cost of a taxi to the airport can be up to 90% more expensive than a bus trip, we recommend that you make use of a taxi as it will not only be faster, but much safer than a bus. The approximate cost of a trip from the center of Cordoba to the airport is ARS 120, and from the airport to the city center cost is approximately ARS 170 , and the difference is that the initial fare from the airport begins at $58, unlike the rest of the city using the day and night rates above.

If you need to know the approximate cost to and from the airport by reference to your hotel, you can get an estimated cost on the following website: http://www.miautobus.com

Please check this site before reaching Cordobaif you need to exchange currencyo to pay for the taxi, so that you can do it for an approximate ammount to what you must pay the taxi. Thus, avoiding changing more money than needed, and then do it as you need in the center of the city, a place that will surely get you better values ​​than at the airport.

It is always advisable to first check the status of flights, and be attentive to any cancellation or schedule change. You can check this information on the website of Aeropuertos Argentina 2000, responsible for administering Cordoba's airport.

Public / urban transport

In Cordoba and in almost every major city in our country, urban transportation does not use cash when paying the ticket, instead a proximity card is used when hopping on the bus.

This card is commonly known as "Red Bus", as it takes it's name from the supplier. You can get and charge your Red Bus card at the Central bus station (Terminal de Colectivos) stores B10 and C19, and at Airport Taravella (1st floor, local "Open 25").

The Red Bus card costs ARS 25; to acquire the user must load it with the amount deemed necessary for travel (without exceeding the technical limit of $ 300). You can use it immediately without having to activate or enable it. The credit load builds up and does not expire.

When charging, the Municipality of Córdoba gives the benefit of an extra 10%, so for example paying ARS 100, an ARS 110 will be available to use in travel. The cost of each trip per passages is ARS 7.15, but in reality and thanks to this 10% benefit, ends up being ARS 6.43.

If the balance on the card is not enough to pay a ticket, it is possible to "travel on credit" up to 2 times with any rate; the amount used will be charged at the next charge.

Unlike other similar systems, the Red Bus card can be borrowed and used by other/s person/s, so if you do not want to keep it, you can pass it to another person. If you are coming come to the event in a group, you can buy a single card and use it for the whole group, thereby reducing costs.

Some city Hostels sell and charge the Red Bus, you can find out before arriving if they provide this service.

Cordoba's Culture

A little about the culture of our city.

Cuarteto

While it may not be the favorite of all people of Cordoba, the "Cuarteto" (quartet) is the popular musical style you will hear in most taxi's, radio or bar during your stay. Carlos "La Mona" Jimenez is one of its greatest references, among others.

Barbecue

As is to be expected in our country, "Asado" (barbecue) occupies a central place in our society, and Choripan (chorizo sandwich) along with Lomito (steak sandwich) are the typical sandwiches. If you want to treat yourself to eat a good Argentine asado, please consult local guides like Yelp or Gastronomic Tour for tips and prices.

What is choripán?

It is a French style bread sandwich stuffed with roasted Creole sausage, accompanied by tomato, lettuce, mayonnaise and several sauces. The best place to try one is "Parque Sarmiento", a green space located next to the "Nueva Cordoba" neighborhood. To give you an idea how important choripán is, for the last 2 years the city has helpd the World Choripán Festival, under the auspices of the Municipality of Cordoba.

Lomito

And if we talk about sandwiches, another classic is a steak one called Lomito. It's a sandwich of white bread stuffed with a piece of sliced beef tenderloin, lettuce, tomato, mayonnaise, fried egg, cheese, seasonings to taste and cooked ham. Always accompanied by french fries.

Fernet

All the prevous can't be alone at a table, so surely you'll have them accompanied by a "Fernet", the preferred drink of Cordoba. It is prepared with fernet + ice + Coca Cola. Experts say the perfect mix is 5% ice + 30% fernet + 65% Coca Cola; those who want to venture a little further, can modify the percentagesto include more fernet in the mix.
We recommend drinking with moderation and responsibly. And never drink if you are going to drive.

Alfajores

The classic souvenir of our lands is the "alfajor serrano", a candy made of two round wafers stuffed with caramel or fruit jams. The most popular course is the chocolate one filled with dulce de leche (caramel).

Weather

During November the climate in Cordoba is mostly warm and humid, with rain probability around 28%, which is typical at this time of year. The temperatures vary between 12°C and 29°C (53.6F - 84.2F), and the average humidity is 55%.

As for clothes, our recommendation is to travel with summer clothes and bring a light or rain jacket in case you need it.

Actividades

Córdoba capital

El Ente de Turismo Ciudad de Córdoba tiene a disposición del turista una serie de recorridos que se pueden realizar de modo autoguiado por los principales atractivos de la ciudad. Pueden obtener las guías aquí

Dependiendo del tipo de turismo y recorrido que quieran hacer, pueden elegir entre los siguientes paseos:

Pueblo Nuevo: Paseo infaltable en la lista de todo visitante que desee conocer el primer barrio obrero de Córdoba. Este circuito recorre la calle Belgrano de Barrio Güemes:, el más bohemio de la ciudad, terminando en el reconocido Paseo de las Artesanías, zona en la cual podemos encontrar desde antigüedades, tortas y comidas típicas hasta los bares y galerías de moda.

Centro Histórico: Punto de partida para recorrer y conocer la ciudad. Desde nuestro Cabildo y su vecina Iglesia Catedral separados por el Pasaje Santa Catalina, testigo de la última dictadura militar que sufrió nuestro país. Todo esto y junto una serie de museos, criptas y basílicas componen este recorrido por la historia de la ciudad.

Córdoba de las Campanas: Un recorrido por la cultura historia de la ciudad, conformada por templos, iglesias y basílicas. Un universo que forman parte de la identidad cordobesa.

Córdoba Jesuítica: Recorre la “Manzana Jesuítica”, declarada Patrimonio Cultural de la Humanidad por UNESCO, maravilla arquitectónica creada por los padres y fundadores de una de las casas de altos estudios más prestigiosas de la región, siendo la universidad más antigua del país y la cuarta en América.

Nueva Córdoba: Este es el barrio de más movimiento en la ciudad, lugar en donde la mayoría de los jóvenes que vienen a cursar sus estudios en Córdoba eligen para vivir. Ha sido también testigo del crecimiento de la ciudad hacia finales del siglo XIX y principio del siglo XX, y es donde podemos hallar gran parte de los museos y atractivos religiosos.

Estos recorridos son un buen punto de partida para conocer Córdoba Capital.

Sierras de Córdoba

La provincia de Córdoba es reconocida por sus sierras y las siguientes son algunas de las ciudades más buscadas y visitadas por los turistas durante todo el año.

Villa General Belgrano

Ciudad ubicada en el Valle de Calamuchita de arquitectura típica bávara y la mayor colonia alemana en toda Argentina, debido a que sus primeros pobladores fueron de ese origen así también como suizo, austríaco e italiano. Es cuna de la fiesta del Chocolate Alpino, la Masa Vienesa y sede argentina de la Oktoberfest.

Para visitar Villa General Belgrano pueden optar por los colectivos de media distancia o minibuses diferenciales que parten con una frecuencia de 45 minutos desde la vieja Terminal de Ómnibus. Las empresas que puede contratar son: Buses Lep, Sierras de Calamuchita o Pájaro Blanco.

http://www.buseslep.com.ar
http://www.sierrasdecalamuchita.com

La Cumbrecita

Es un pueblo cuyas casas se ubican a orillas de los senderos que atraviesan el bosque que se encuentra en un pequeño valle de las Sierras Grandes. A una altura de 1450 msnm, La Cumbrecita es el paseo ideal para realizar durante el día, y este pueblo peatonal es testigo del equilibrio obtenido entre la naturaleza y la mano del hombre.

El acceso al pueblo está totalmente asfaltado por lo que pueden llegar en auto o en bus con la empresa Pájaro Blanco. Cabe aclarar que este es un pueblo peatonal, donde está prohibido el ingreso de vehículos, razón por la todos sus recorridos han sido pensados para realizar a pie.

Importante: en este pueblo no hay estaciones de servicio donde cargar combustible, tampoco bancos ni cajeros automáticos, por lo que deberán tener esto en cuenta antes de visitarlo.

Desde La Cumbrecita se puede llegar al complejo Peñón del Águila, lugar perfecto para ponernos al día con nuestra adrenalina.
Parque temático “Peñón del Águila”: http://www.penondelaguila.com.ar

Alta Gracia

Continuando con la huella dejada en nuestra provincia por la Compañía de Jesús, se puede recorrer el Museo Nacional Estancia Jesuítica de Alta Gracia y posterior Casa del Virrey Liniers. Otro de los atractivos de la ciudad es el Museo Casa de Ernesto "Che" Guevara, vivienda que fuese habitada por el “Che” durante su infancia y adolescencia.

A la ciudad se puede llegar en auto o en bus con la compañía Sierras de Calamuchita.

Villa Carlos Paz

Carlos Paz es una de la ciudades preferidas para veranear, no solo por los cordobeses sino por personas de todo el país. Es también una de las plazas teatrales más importantes del país.

En verano es conocido por sus playas a orillas del río San Antonio y por sus obras de teatro. Durante el día o la noche podemos caminar por la costanera que es un paseo a la vera del río y el lago que une el centro de la ciudad con los puentes, el Paseo de los Patos, los clubes náuticos, balnearios, campings, el Centro de Convenciones y el Polideportivo. Otro de sus atractivos turísticos principales es el reloj Cu-Cú, inaugurado el 25 de mayo de 1958, mide 7 m de altura. El mismo posee un gran pájaro Cu-Cú de madera se asoma para cantar las horas y las medias horas, acompañado por un "gong" gigante. Con el paso del tiempo se ha transformado en un icono turístico de la ciudad. No olviden sacarse una foto aquí.

Se puede llegar en auto o bus con las empresas FONOBUS o Sarmiento.
